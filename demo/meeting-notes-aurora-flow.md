# Campaign kickoff — Aurora Flow launch
**Date:** July 14, 2026
**Attendees:** Sam (Marketing Manager), Priya (Brand), Diego (Social), Kat (E-comm), Leo (Finance, partial)

## Context
- Launching the new **Aurora Flow** collection (recycled-fabric leggings + tops) in the **US** in late September, exact date TBC pending supply confirmation.
- Big push this quarter is partnerships — Sam wants us to stop relying on paid social alone. Goal for this campaign: **drive awareness and first-time purchases among younger wellness-focused shoppers**, not just our existing athlete base.
- Leo: budget envelope roughly **$35–45k for creator/partner fees**, final number needs sign-off from finance next cycle.

## What we know / believe
- Our best customers are performance-driven, but growth is probably in the more casual wellness crowd — people doing challenges, run clubs, "movement as self-care" types. Priya thinks women 20–40 is the core for Flow.
- Brand values to protect in any partnership: **performance credibility, inclusivity, sustainability**. Priya: "no partner who's had greenwashing drama, the recycled-fabric story has to be authentic."
- Diego: short-form video is where our engagement is — TikTok and Reels. Our YouTube is basically dormant, don't build the campaign around it.
- Kat: Flow pre-waitlist skews new visitors, which supports the acquisition angle.

## Creative direction (early)
- Lean into authentic training/wellness moments, not polished studio shoots.
- UGC-style content preferred; partners should show the product in real routines.
- Sustainability story should be present but not preachy — "show the fabric, don't lecture."

## Constraints
- No exclusivity deals longer than one campaign cycle at this stage.
- Legal wants standard disclosure compliance (#ad) on all partner content.
- Avoid partners currently working with direct activewear competitors.

## Open items
- Final launch date (supply chain confirmation due ~Aug 1).
- Exact budget split between hero partners vs micro/community partners.
- Whether we include a UK soft-launch this cycle — parked for now, US only.
- Measurement plan beyond promo-code redemptions — Kat to propose.
